My Love-Hate Relationship with the Cardinals and Blues

2011 St. Louis Cardinals, Best Year of My Life

In baseball, there are 162 games that make up a season. Not very often does it take all 162 to determine if your gonna make the playoffs but this year was one of them. Chris Carpenter pitched a complete game shutout and the Cardinals did their job and won 8-0 against Houston. The Atlanta Braves needed to lose and in the bottom of the 13th, the Atlanta Braves did just that. All the Cardinals players were watching the t.v in the clubhouse jumping up and down, hugging and crying after realizing they made it to the playoffs.

Once in the postseason, we defeated the Philidephia Phillies in a thrilling 5 game series that introduced the Rally Squirrel. The next we defeated the Milwaukee Brewers and David Freese was the well deserved MVP of the series. Now in the World Series, the Texas Rangers only stand in our way of becoming Champions of Baseball.

Game 6 of the 2011 World Series, the greatest game of baseball to have ever been played, if your a Cardinals fan. A back and forth battle all night came down to the bottom of the night, 2 on and 2out, 2strikes. 1 more stike its over and 1 swing could win it. David Freese ends up hitting a triple to tie the game and send it into extra innings. The top of the 10th Josh Hamiliton hit a two-run homerun and all hope seemed lost. In the bottom of the 10th, 2 on, 2out, 2 strikes, down 8-9, the pitch and swing its a basehit into centerfield! Tie game 9-9. A scorless top of the 11th lead to the bottom of the night, first man up is previous hero David Freese. David works the count to 3-2 and then the call "Freese, hits it in the air to center. We will see you, tomorrow night!"

Chris Carpenter takes the ball in game 7 and before you know it Jason Motte is pitching in the 9th and Allen Craig records the final out. The 2011 St.Louis Cardinals have won the World Series!

Some of the Blues Greatest Moments I Have Seen

As much as I love the blues, I had to have nhl.com assist me in remembering some of the details of these games. The first two bullet points are from the website, but even a detailed description of those great nights wont do it justice. I am a nervous wreck, I pace back and forth, I cry, I yell, I scream and in the end im exhausted from the roller coaster of emotions.

  • April 25, 2016 - The Blues are locked in a 2-2 tie with the defending Stanley Cup Champion Chicago Blackhawks in Game 7 of the Western Conference quarterfinal. With 12:29 left in the period, Blues forward Troy Brouwer banged home a loose puck in the crease, sending the Blues to a big victory over their biggest rivals. The Blues would eventually reach the Western Conference Final for the first time in 15 years.
  • November 29, 2000 - The Blues came back from a 5-0 deficit in the third period to beat the Toronto Maple Leafs 6-5 in overtime. It was only the second time a team has come back such a large deficit in the third period to win a game. The Blues scored their six goals in 15 minutes and 27 seconds, making it the fastest time a team has come back from a five-goal deficit in NHL history.
  • Tarasenko scored one of the greatest goals of all time Tarasenko's Great Goal
